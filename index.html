// Protocol

How it works

01

You write

Type your message. Your browser locks it with a fresh key on the spot — that key never leaves your device.

02

You seal it

One signature turns your message into a sealed drop a promise Onchain that it exists, waiting for exactly one person.

03

Held on BOT Chain

The drop lives Onchain, not on a server. Nobody can peek, edit, or quietly delete it before it's read.

04

They open it — once

Your person connects their wallet, claims it, reads it. The moment they do, it's closed for good.

// The promise

What can go wrong?

READ TWICE

Can they screenshot and reread it later?

Once opened, does it just sit there forever?

One claim, enforced Onchain. After that, it's closed, no replays.
HANDLED
SHUTDOWN

What if the app goes down?

Servers get taken down, companies fold, links rot.

The drop isn't stored on our server. It's onchain, the app is just how you read it.
HANDLED
WRONG PERSON

What if someone else gets the link?

Forwarded links, shared devices, wrong copy-paste.

The message is locked to a key that only decrypts client-side. A link alone isn't enough.
HANDLED
TAMPERING

Could the message get changed after sending?

Edited mid-flight, swapped, altered before it's read.

Every drop is checked against its onchain commitment before it's ever decrypted.

// Properties

Built different.

One Read, No Exceptions

Every drop is claimed once and only once — enforced onchain, not by an app policy someone could quietly change.

No Backend

This entire dapp is a static file. Drops live on BOT Chain. There's no server to subpoena, hack, or shut down.

Public Drop Links

Post your drop link anywhere. Anyone can send you a message without an account — just a wallet and a moment.

Open Protocol

DEADROP is a convention, not a platform. The contract is public. Anyone can build a compatible sender or reader. It's yours.
